My initial reactions (Section 1a)

What emotions/feelings does this performance invoke? What is your initial reaction?

My initial reaction was joy. I felt happy because you could hear the singers very clearly, and they were in sync. Even when the lower part got quieter, you could still hear them clearly.

What do you think the purpose of this song is?

I think the purpose of this song was for entertainment. I know Inuit singers perform throat singing as a form of entertainment and contest, to see who can outlast the other.

In what setting do you think the song would be performed?

I think this is a song that would be performed in a friend/family gathering. I think this because throat singing traditionally is less of a music genre, and more of a game.

Where (geographically) do you think this music is from and what made you think it was from this area?

I think this music is from Canada. I know this because there are two people singing, which is how Inuit throat singing is performed.

Listening for the elements of music (Section 1b)

Melody Harmonies Texture
The melody is performed by one voice the entire time. The melodic singer is singing less gutturally than the lower singer, but still using a throat singing voice. I think the melody is in a major key. There are multiple pitches moving together, there’s a low pitch and a high pitch. They’re in harmony because the lower pitch acts as a percussion track for the higher pitch singer. I hear two sounds. The sound is very thick with the lower singer, while the higher singer has a more medium texture.
Tone/Timbre Dynamics Tempo
Both singers have a very warm sound. The lower singer has a more warm and heavy tone, I think this is because they’re singing very gutturally from their throat, which produces a low warm sound. The higher singer is less guttural and is more melodic. The piece changes dynamics a lot. The volume in the singers fluctuate as the lower singer gets louder and the high singer gets quieter. I think it’s mostly mezzo-forte. This piece is fairly fast, probably around allegro. The piece gets faster about halfway through.
